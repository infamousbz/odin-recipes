Lasagna

Description

This mouthwatering lasagna is a hearty and indulgent Italian classic, beautifully plated on a sleek black dish.
Layers of tender pasta sheets are generously filled with rich, savory meat sauce and smothered in a velvety béchamel.
The golden-brown crust of bubbling, melted cheese on top adds the perfect finishing touch, while a sprig of fresh basil
crowns the dish, offering a burst of aromatic freshness.The contrast of textures—from the creamy, cheesy top to the
flavorful, meaty interior—makes this lasagna a comforting and satisfying meal that’s as visually inviting as it is delicious.

Ingredients

  • 9–12 lasagna noodles (boiled or oven-ready)
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (or beef/pork mix)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped;3 cloves garlic, minced;1 tsp dried oreganooregano;1 tsp dried basil.
  • 24 oz (680g) tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 15 oz (425g) ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • (Optional) 2 cups béchamel sauce (butter, flour, milk)

Steps to cook the recipe

  1. Prepare the meat sauce
    • Heat a tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
    • Add chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
    • Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
    • Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
    • Stir in the tomato sauce, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
    • Simmer uncovered for 15–20 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  2. Prepare the Ricotta layer
    • In a bowl, mix ricotta cheesericotta cheese with a pinch of salt and pepper. You can also add 1 beaten egg for richness (optional)
  3. Preheat the oven
    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Assemble the Lasagna
    • In a 9x13" baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
    • Add a layer of lasagna noodles.
    • Spread a layer of ricotta mixture.
    • Spoon on some meat sauce.
    • Sprinkle a layer of mozzarella and Parmesan.
    • Repeat layers (noodles → ricotta → sauce → cheese) until all ingredients are used. Top with mozzarella and Parmesan.
    • (Optional) If using béchamel, drizzle it over each layer before adding cheese.
  5. Bake
    • Cover the dish with foil (tent it slightly so it doesn't stick to cheese).
    • Bake for 25 minutes, then remove foil and bake an additional 15–20 minutes, until the top is bubbly and golden.
  6. Rest and Serve
    • Let lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before cutting—this helps it set.
    • Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve warm!
