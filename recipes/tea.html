Indian Chai

Description

This image beautifully captures a calming moment with a warm cup of herbal tea served in a modern,
double-walled glass cup. The tea has a rich amber hue, hinting at its depth of flavor and natural goodness.
It is placed on a rustic jute mat, accompanied by vibrant yellow flowers that add a cheerful touch to the serene
setup. The soft natural lighting and the earthy background evoke a sense of peace, making it perfect for a relaxing
afternoon or a mindful tea ritual.

Ingredients

  • Dried or fresh yellow flowers (such as calendula, chamomile, or marigold)
  • Hot water
  • Honey or natural sweetener (optional)
  • Lemon slices or juice (optional, for added flavor and health benefits)

Steps to make Indian Chai

  1. Boil the water
    • Heat water until just below boiling. Avoid fully boiling to preserve the delicate flavor of the flowers.
  2. Prepare the tea flowers
    • Place dried or fresh flowers into a tea infuser, tea bag, or directly into a cup or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water
    • Gently pour hot water over the flowers.
  4. Steep the tea
    • Let it steep for 5â€“10 minutes depending on how strong you like your tea. Cover the cup to retain the essential oils and aroma.
  5. Strain (if needed)
    • If flowers were added loose, strain the tea into another cup.
  6. Add honey or lemon
    • Stir in honey or a squeeze of lemon for added sweetness and zest (optional).
  7. Serve & enjoy
    • Sip slowly and enjoy your calming herbal tea.
