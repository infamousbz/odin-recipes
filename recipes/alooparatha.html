Aloo Paratha

Description

This image showcases a delicious serving of traditional Indian Aloo Paratha,
a beloved North Indian flatbread stuffed with a flavorful spiced potato filling.
The golden-brown parathas are perfectly crisp on the outside with specks of char,
indicating expert pan-frying. Garnished with fresh herbs and sesame seeds, the parathas
are served hot on a thali with tangy pickle, cool and creamy curd topped with red chili powder,
and accompanied by refreshing sprigs of mint.This dish is a staple of Indian breakfasts and is equally popular for lunch or dinner, offering a satisfying blend of textures and bold, earthy flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Water (as needed)
  • 1 tsp oil or gheeghee (optional, for softer dough)
  • 3–4 medium boiled potatoes, peeled and mashed
  • 1–2 green chilieschilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds or ajwain (carom seeds)
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp red chili powder;½ tsp garam masala;½ tsp amchur powder or juice of ½ lemon
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
  • Salt, to taste
  • Gheeor oil for roasting

Steps to cook the recipe

  1. Make the Dough
    • In a large bowl, mix whole wheat flour and salt.
    • Slowly add water, kneading into a soft, smooth dough.
    • Optionally, add a little oil or ghee while kneading.
    • Cover and let the dough rest for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Aloo Filling
    • In a mixing bowl, add mashed potatoes
    • Mix in green chilies, ginger, cumin/ajwain, turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, amchur/lemon juice, and chopped coriander.
    • Add salt to taste and mix thoroughly. The filling should be lump-free.
  3. Assemble the Parathas
    • Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
    • Roll out one dough ball into a small circle (about 4–5 inches).
    • Place a spoonful of the potato filling in the center.
    • Bring the edges together to seal the filling and pinch off excess dough at the top.
    • Gently flatten and roll out again into a 6–7 inch paratha using light pressure.
  4. Cook the Parathas
    • Heat a tawa or skillet on medium heat.
    • Place the rolled paratha on the hot tawa.
    • Cook one side for 30–40 seconds, then flip.
    • Apply ghee/oil on the cooked side, flip again, and repeat.
    • Cook until both sides are golden brown with crisp spots.
  5. Serve Hot
    • Serve your hot Aloo Parathas with curd/yogurt, pickle, butter, or a hot glass of masala chai!.
